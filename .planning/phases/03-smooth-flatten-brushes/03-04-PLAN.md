  <name>UAT: Smooth and Flatten Brush Modes</name>
  <what-built>
    Three brush modes cycling with M: Raise/Lower (existing), Smooth (Gaussian average), Flatten (samples height on first press, levels while held). HUD shows active mode.
  </what-built>
  <how-to-verify>
    Commit all changes and wait for laptop watcher to pull and relaunch Godot.

    **1. Mode Cycling (M key)**
    - Press F3 to enter editor.
    - Confirm HUD shows "[Raise/Lower]" in amber below the coordinates.
    - Press M once → HUD shows "[Smooth]".
    - Press M again → HUD shows "[Flatten]".
    - Press M again → HUD shows "[Raise/Lower]" (wraps correctly).

    **2. Smooth Brush**
    - In Raise/Lower mode, hold LMB and rapidly paint a spiky hill (use R key for boost).
    - Press M to switch to Smooth mode.
    - Hold LMB over the spiky area.
    - After 1-2 seconds the spikes should visibly reduce — the surface becomes rounder.
    - Check: RMB in Smooth mode does nothing.
    - Check: R key while smoothing noticeably speeds up convergence.

    **3. Flatten Brush**
    - Press M to switch to Flatten mode.
    - Move cursor to a flat area of the terrain.
    - Press and hold LMB — a flat zone should form and spread under the cursor.
    - Release LMB. Move cursor to a raised hill.
    - Press and hold LMB again — the hill should flatten, but to the hill's height (new sample), NOT to the previous flat area's height.
    - Release LMB, move cursor somewhere else, press again — confirms new sample each time.
    - Check: the resulting flat patch looks driveable (no spikes, uniform height).
    - Check: RMB in Flatten mode does nothing.

    **4. FPS Check**
    - While smoothing or flattening at maximum radius (scroll wheel up), confirm FPS stays 30+ (check in-game FPS counter if available, or judge by frame smoothness).
  </how-to-verify>
  <resume-signal>Type "approved" if all checks pass, or describe which checks failed and what you observed.</resume-signal>
